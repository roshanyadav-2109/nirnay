Nirnay — Demo Video Script

Every verdict, with evidence.
Theme 3 · CRPF Procurement  |  PanIIT AI for Bharat Hackathon 2026
Presenter: Roshan Yadav  |  Target runtime: 2 min 30 s

How to read this script. Italic blocks on a parchment background are the lines you speak — straight to camera, conversational, with the small pauses written in. Light-blue blocks are visual cues: what's on screen, what to click, where to look. Timestamps are guides, not handcuffs — if a beat lands, give it room.

1. Opening — on camera, looking at lens

[0:00 – 0:15]

“Hi, I'm Roshan.

Before I show you what I built, let me ask you something. Imagine you're a CRPF procurement officer. A tender lands on your desk — a hundred-page PDF, fifteen bidders, each with eight to ten supporting documents. Your job? Decide who's eligible. And defend that decision in court, sometimes years later.”

Direction. Pause here. Let it breathe. Slight shift in tone before the next line.

“Right now, that takes officers about two weeks per tender. I built something that does it in under a minute — and unlike every other AI tool out there, mine can actually be defended in court. It's called Nirnay — Hindi for verdict. And it's built specifically for Theme 3.”

2. The problem

[0:15 – 0:35]
Cut to. Screen recording starts. Briefly: B-roll of a desk with scattered PDFs, then transition into the dashboard.

“Here's what makes this hard. Existing AI tools give you a black-box yes-or-no. An officer can't walk into a courtroom and say ‘the AI said so.’ That's not a defence — that's a liability.

The other option? Build an OCR pipeline with LayoutLM, PaddleOCR, six months of fine-tuning. Defence Ministry timelines don't work that way.

So I asked one question: what would a system look like that an officer could actually trust? Two non-negotiables fell out of that question.”

3. The two principles

[0:35 – 0:55]
On screen. Clean text overlay, two principles side by side. Simple typography, lots of whitespace.

“One — every verdict points at evidence. Not a number floating in a dashboard. The exact quote, the exact document, the exact page number. If the system says a bidder's turnover is five crore, you can click it and see the line on the CA certificate that proves it.

Two — Nirnay never silently rejects. If evidence is missing or unclear, the verdict is needs_review. Never not_eligible. Because rejecting the wrong bidder costs the government legal cases and reputation. A human review costs ten minutes.”

4. The demo — live walkthrough

[0:55 – 1:45]
Cut to app. Dashboard view. Move slowly — the click-to-evidence moment is the hero of the whole pitch. Don't rush.

“Okay, let me show you. (Open dashboard.) This is a real tender. Five bidders, ten eligibility criteria already extracted by Gemini 2.5 Flash — directly from the PDF, no OCR pipeline.

(Click Evaluation page.) I hit Run Evaluation. Watch this — two seconds, all five bidders evaluated against all ten criteria. That's fifty verdicts.

(Click on Sharma Construction → eligible.) Eligible. But here's the thing — (click a verdict) — every single verdict opens an evidence panel. Look. The exact quoted line from their CA certificate. Document name. Page number. Confidence score. Reasoning. An officer can take a screenshot of this and put it on file.

(Click Gupta Builders → not eligible.) This one's rejected. Why? Their turnover is 3.4 crore. The tender requires 5. Look — (point to evidence) — the source line is right there. Defensible.

(Click National Infrastructure → needs review.) And this is the one I'm proudest of. The system doesn't know. Their ISO certificate expires on the bid date. Is that valid or not? The rules are ambiguous. So Nirnay refuses to guess — it flags it for an officer to decide. That's the rule: when in doubt, ask a human.”

5. The audit chain — the killer demo

[1:45 – 2:10]
Hero shot. Pause for a beat after “Caught.” Let the red row do the work. This is the moment the pitch lands.

“Now here's the part I think judges will care about most.

(Open Audit page.) Every action in the system — every upload, every verdict, every override — goes into a hash-chained audit log. SHA-256, computed in the browser. Sixty-plus events here.

(Click ‘Simulate tampering’.) Watch — I'm going to corrupt one row. Directly via SQL. Like an attacker would.

(Click ‘Verify Chain’.) One click. Caught. Red row. Exact event flagged. This is what makes a verdict survive a court challenge five years from now.”

6. Closing — back on camera

[2:10 – 2:30]

“So that's Nirnay.

One officer, one tender — two weeks of work, done in minutes. A hundred percent citation coverage. A tamper-proof audit trail. Built on free-tier Gemini, deployed as a static site, costs less than fifty rupees per tender to run.

If a CRPF directorate processes a hundred and twenty tenders a year, that's twelve thousand four hundred officer-hours redirected away from paperwork — and into the work that actually needs human judgement.

I'm Roshan Yadav. This is Nirnay. Every verdict, with evidence.

Thank you.”

7. Delivery notes for recording

  • Pace yourself on the demo segment. The temptation is to rush. Don't. The “click and see evidence” moment is the entire pitch in one beat — let it land.
  • The tampering demo is your hero shot. Pause for half a second after “Caught.” The red row is doing the talking; you don't have to.
  • Speak like you're explaining it to a friend who's smart but tired. Not a panel. Not a marketing video. Keep contractions (I'm, doesn't); keep the small pauses already written in.
  • Theme 3 callout sits in the opening line on purpose. Don't bury it later.
  • If you go over time, the easiest cut is the closing economics paragraph (the 12,400 officer-hours line). It's nice-to-have, not load-bearing.
  • Camera setup. Eye-level. Soft front light, no harsh shadow. Plain, slightly out-of-focus background — not a blank wall.
  • Audio matters more than video. If you can borrow a lavalier or a decent USB mic, do it. Phone audio reads as “hackathon submission”; clean audio reads as “founder.”
